4. New Issues Discovered in Audit 3 (8)

Critical Contact form is non-functional — no action attribute

The <form> element on /contact has no action attribute and no JavaScript submit handler. The submit button renders and appears interactive but the form submits to nowhere. For any visitor who fills out the form, their message is lost. This is the most functionally broken element on the site.

Fix: Add a form handler. Quickest options: Netlify Forms (add netlify attribute), Formspree (action="https://formspree.io/f/YOUR_ID"), or a Vercel serverless function.

Critical Address contradiction — "Singapore" (meta) vs "London UK" (contact page)

The homepage meta description reads "Based in Singapore, working globally." The contact page shows "123 Design Street, Studio 42, London UK" — a recognisably fictional placeholder address. This is a two-continent contradiction. Google's entity resolution, AI citation engines, and human prospects doing due diligence will all flag this. The address has now changed in every audit (KL Malaysia → London UK) without the Singapore meta claim being updated.

Fix: Decide on a real address (or "Remote-first studio, APAC-focused"). Update: (1) homepage meta description, (2) contact page copy, (3) Organization schema address property, (4) LocalBusiness schema on /contact. All four must be consistent.

High Testimonial/team name collision — Sarah Jenkins and David Miller

"Sarah Jenkins" appears as the agency's Head of Design on /about and as "CEO at Vercel" in the homepage testimonial slider. "David Miller" appears as the agency's Lead Developer on /about and as "CTO at Linear" in the testimonials. Any CMO or analyst doing due diligence will notice this within seconds — it reads as fabricated social proof. The GEO agent also flagged this as an entity resolution risk for AI citation systems.

Fix: Either (a) rename the testimonial attributions to names not used in the team roster, or (b) replace the placeholder testimonials with real client quotes. File: src/pages/index.astro lines 38–57.

High Homepage slider "View Case Study" buttons link nowhere

The work slider cards on the homepage each show a "View Case Study" arrow/button. Clicking does not navigate anywhere — the links have no href. This breaks the most important micro-conversion on the site: a prospect sees compelling work, clicks to learn more, and hits a dead end. The /work page's meta description now promises "six case studies" — but neither the slider nor /work delivers case-study content.

Fix (short-term): Link each slider card to its corresponding card on /work. Fix (proper): Create individual case study pages or expandable sections with challenge/solution/results copy.

High Organization logo schema points to favicon.svg

The Organization.logo.url in the homepage JSON-LD points to favicon.svg (~749 bytes, 16×16 or 32×32px icon). Google's structured data guidelines require the logo to be a proper image at least 160×90px in a standard raster format (PNG/JPG). An SVG favicon does not satisfy this requirement and may prevent Knowledge Panel logo eligibility.

Fix: Point Organization.logo.url to /og-image.jpg (once it exists) or a dedicated /logo.png at minimum 160×90px.

High Team LinkedIn links all go to the company page, not individual profiles

All three LinkedIn links on /about (Marcus Chen, Sarah Jenkins, David Miller) resolve to linkedin.com/company/innovate — the same company page URL for all three people. A CMO clicking to verify individual credentials finds a company page and cannot confirm any individual person exists. This actively undermines the team credibility that was the main content improvement of this audit cycle.

Fix: Link to actual LinkedIn personal profiles, or remove the LinkedIn links if the profiles don't exist rather than leaving placeholder company page URLs.

Medium Title tag encoding error — mojibake em-dash on /about and /contact

The em-dash character (—) in the title tags "About Innovate — B2B Digital Agency Since 2018" and "Contact Innovate — Start Your Project" renders as â€" (UTF-8 mojibake) in some parsers. This can corrupt SERP snippet display in certain search result contexts. Root cause: likely a character encoding mismatch in the Astro build output.

Fix: Replace the em-dash with an ASCII hyphen-minus (-) in title strings, or verify the Astro build is outputting UTF-8 consistently.

Medium FAQ schema answers truncated — shorter than visible HTML copy

The FAQPage JSON-LD answers (17–19 words each) are shorter than the already-short visible FAQ copy (23–28 words each). Google and AI citation engines use the schema answer text, not the visible text. The target for AI citation eligibility is 134–167 words per answer. Current answers are at 10–13% of that minimum. The schema is actively summarising away the small amount of content that exists.

Fix: At minimum, sync the schema acceptedAnswer.text with the full visible answer text. Better: expand answers to 60–100 words each. File: src/pages/index.astro, FAQPage JSON-LD block.

High loading="lazy" + fetchpriority="high" conflict — still present 5 min

The "Our Expertise" section <img> still has both loading="lazy" and fetchpriority="high". This conflict migrated from the hero image (now removed) to the next visible image. loading="lazy" wins in most browser implementations, making fetchpriority="high" non-functional. Since this image is below the fold, both attributes are wrong — use loading="lazy" only, remove fetchpriority="high".

Medium Fade-up elements without SSR is-visible may cause CLS 30 min

The hero element correctly has is-visible pre-applied in SSR HTML. However, other .fade-up-element nodes on the page do not have is-visible at server-render time. These elements start in a hidden/transformed state and shift into position after JS hydration. If hydration is delayed, these shifts register as CLS.
